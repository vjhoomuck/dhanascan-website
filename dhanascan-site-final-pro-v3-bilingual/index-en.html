DhanaScan philosophy

Growing toward understanding

A banana naturally curves as it grows toward the light. I imagined DhanaScan with the same idea: guiding children step by step toward understanding in a natural, simple and reassuring way.

A simple method

How does it work?

A clear progression from sounds to words, then to short sentences.

1. Print

Prepare the cards and booklets from the Resources section.

2. Scan

The child scans a Dhana card with the app.

3. Repeat

The child listens to the sound and repeats at their own pace.

4. Build words

Syllables combine to build simple words.

5. Read sentences

Step by step, the child progresses toward short sentences.

DhanaScan combines interactive learning, printable resources and hands-on activities.

Phonics cards

Dhana cards are designed to be recognized by the app and help children practice sounds step by step.

Activity booklets

All phonics booklets follow the same visual learning universe: alphabet tracing, progressive phonics learning, writing practice, hands-on activities and coloring.

Building words

The child combines sounds to understand how syllables build words. Example: BA → Banana, NA → Napkin, NA → Napkin, then BA.NA.NA.

Simple stories

Short stories help children connect sounds, words and meaning. Example: DO.DI HAS A BA.NA.NA.

About

A mother developer behind DhanaScan

DhanaScan was created by Vandhana Teeluckdharry Jhoomuck, a mother and independent developer.

Seen in the Fontenay-le-Fleury magazine

A project born from real learning experiences with children

DhanaScan grew from hands-on work around simple sounds, educational cards and progressive reading discovery.

The goal is to offer a reassuring, playful and accessible approach to help young children discover sounds and first words from age 3.

Frequently asked questions

DhanaScan FAQ

What age is DhanaScan recommended for?

DhanaScan is mainly designed for children ages 3+. Older children who need support with reading may also use it with adult guidance.

Which languages are available?

French, English and Hindi are currently available. More English and Hindi content is being added progressively.

Is DhanaScan free?

Yes. The DhanaScan app can be downloaded for free. Some additional resources and in-app content are available at affordable prices.

Is there a subscription?

No. DhanaScan does not require a monthly subscription.

Do paid resources and YouTube episodes require parental permission?

Yes. Paid resources and YouTube episodes should be accessed with parental permission and supervision.

Does DhanaScan work with other cards?

No. DhanaScan works only with official Dhana educational cards designed for the app.

Can the booklets be printed multiple times?

Booklets can be printed up to 3 times for personal family or educational use.

Are the resources protected?

Yes. All DhanaScan resources, including cards, booklets, stories, illustrations and educational content, are protected by copyright.

Does the app collect personal data?

No. DhanaScan does not collect children’s personal data. There are no ads and no child account.

What is the camera used for?

The camera is used only to scan Dhana educational cards. Images are not sent to a server.

My child lacks vocabulary. What can I do?

You can also explore DhanaMoriah, an app designed to enrich children’s vocabulary through images, words and simple activities.

Are there other Dhana apps?

Yes. The Dhana universe also includes:

  • DhanaStories — educational stories for children
  • DhanaTaleStories — narrated stories and coloring activities
  • DhanaVerbeFrançais — learning French verbs
  • DhanaRangDe — creative activities for children
  • DhanaMathSmart — learning mathematics through simple and playful activities
